# Balcony Shoe Box

I made a shoe box! I plan to put it on my balcony to protect my balcony slippers from the rain and dust. I designed a slanted lid so that the rain can drip down instead of accumulating on top of the box.

To make the shoe box, I first scrapped out some material. I found a 6-inch x 7/8 inch yellow pine wood strip and some pieces of plywood from the pile. The plywood pieces have grooves on them already from their original project.

Fun fact: the yellow pine boards are originally from the old floorings in MIT Building 4, and the plywood boards are from the cabinets in the student center.

![PXL_20210623_202921593.jpeg](https://s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/secure.notion-static.com/0ba9fefd-cd05-4225-9b56-b48f41f09208/PXL_20210623_202921593.jpeg?X-Amz-Algorithm=AWS4-HMAC-SHA256&X-Amz-Credential=AKIAT73L2G45O3KS52Y5%2F20210906%2Fus-west-2%2Fs3%2Faws4_request&X-Amz-Date=20210906T014546Z&X-Amz-Expires=86400&X-Amz-Signature=22942ee60bda05b1f4e63473853d711a72e3145d9467d5454aba9bbede8fb717&X-Amz-SignedHeaders=host&response-content-disposition=filename%20%3D%22PXL_20210623_202921593.jpeg%22)

I first made the front and back panels with yellow pine, where I glued the side-grains together.

![PXL_20210623_195816900.jpeg](https://s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/secure.notion-static.com/45b6abdf-cf6a-42fd-9fa5-936396a9eef4/PXL_20210623_195816900.jpeg?X-Amz-Algorithm=AWS4-HMAC-SHA256&X-Amz-Credential=AKIAT73L2G45O3KS52Y5%2F20210906%2Fus-west-2%2Fs3%2Faws4_request&X-Amz-Date=20210906T014602Z&X-Amz-Expires=86400&X-Amz-Signature=e6e3fbf7bceb09c129ae8a50ea4bced61b968559646b9a22cdec96aa4d489501&X-Amz-SignedHeaders=host&response-content-disposition=filename%20%3D%22PXL_20210623_195816900.jpeg%22)

To account for the slanted lid, I made one side of them slanted with a table saw. Since I planned to use dadoes to hold the bottom panel, I then cut the grooves for the dadoes with a half-inch router.

![PXL_20210625_190853911.jpeg](https://s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/secure.notion-static.com/de27b8d9-6efe-41ac-969b-337c292b55b9/PXL_20210625_190853911.jpeg?X-Amz-Algorithm=AWS4-HMAC-SHA256&X-Amz-Credential=AKIAT73L2G45O3KS52Y5%2F20210906%2Fus-west-2%2Fs3%2Faws4_request&X-Amz-Date=20210906T014620Z&X-Amz-Expires=86400&X-Amz-Signature=b5b365c3573f87d8e87c20a2bd19c347ca18ec852b53d9552f2b974c33de7053&X-Amz-SignedHeaders=host&response-content-disposition=filename%20%3D%22PXL_20210625_190853911.jpeg%22)

I also cut some rabbets on the yellow pine panels so that the joints are stronger. I then slid the bottom panel in and glued everything together.

![PXL_20210707_195250253.jpg](https://s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/secure.notion-static.com/60276247-45c9-427b-a420-2a87c0c3fcde/PXL_20210707_195250253.jpg?X-Amz-Algorithm=AWS4-HMAC-SHA256&X-Amz-Credential=AKIAT73L2G45O3KS52Y5%2F20210906%2Fus-west-2%2Fs3%2Faws4_request&X-Amz-Date=20210906T014714Z&X-Amz-Expires=86400&X-Amz-Signature=5109a9ff18b0e8af096a872f531c0d5d5053cb8619b4ce84cf480b587c6a66d0&X-Amz-SignedHeaders=host&response-content-disposition=filename%20%3D%22PXL_20210707_195250253.jpg%22)

I also cut a strip of yellow pine as an inlay on the existing grooves of the lid.

![PXL_20210707_205328121.jpg](https://s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/secure.notion-static.com/8cdc2f5f-dc3d-4f94-8605-c4907fd32e1f/PXL_20210707_205328121.jpg?X-Amz-Algorithm=AWS4-HMAC-SHA256&X-Amz-Credential=AKIAT73L2G45O3KS52Y5%2F20210906%2Fus-west-2%2Fs3%2Faws4_request&X-Amz-Date=20210906T014731Z&X-Amz-Expires=86400&X-Amz-Signature=fa1146cf47fbd5eeded6e1bb3ecc4adaf2af45e16fecd4607c1f3cc2df4718da&X-Amz-SignedHeaders=host&response-content-disposition=filename%20%3D%22PXL_20210707_205328121.jpg%22)

I then chiseled some space for the hinges, and used the hinges to connect the lid and the box.

![PXL_20210707_184536730.jpg](https://s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/secure.notion-static.com/c27f17a4-a775-48a8-99bd-58be36dbb97e/PXL_20210707_184536730.jpg?X-Amz-Algorithm=AWS4-HMAC-SHA256&X-Amz-Credential=AKIAT73L2G45O3KS52Y5%2F20210906%2Fus-west-2%2Fs3%2Faws4_request&X-Amz-Date=20210906T014747Z&X-Amz-Expires=86400&X-Amz-Signature=5b22f7a8007f2e5c17acaa18c2712803369c582d0275469db59707b8e5865433&X-Amz-SignedHeaders=host&response-content-disposition=filename%20%3D%22PXL_20210707_184536730.jpg%22)

Now the box is ready. :)
